Recipe for Chicken Dum Biriyani!!

  1. In a large bowl, combine the chicken, ginger garlic paste, coriander powder, cumin powder, garam masala powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well to coat the chicken.
  2. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides.
  3. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the tomatoes and cook until softened, about 5 minutes more.
  5. Stir in the rice, yogurt, cilantro, and mint.
  6. Pour in enough water to cover the rice by 1 inch. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through.
  7. Remove from heat and let stand for 5 minutes before serving.

Making best pizza!!!

  1. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, yeast, and salt
  2. Add the olive oil and warm water, and stir until a dough forms.
  3. Knead the dough on a lightly floured surface for 5-10 minutes, until smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with plastic wrap, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Preheat your oven to 500 degrees F (260 degrees C).
  6. Grease a pizza pan or baking sheet.
  7. Punch down the dough and roll it out into a 12-inch circle.
  8. Transfer the dough to the prepared pan or baking sheet.
  9. Top the pizza with your desired toppings.
  10. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted.

Making best Kolkata style chicken Biriyani

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 1 minute more.
  4. Add the turmeric powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, red chili powder, garam masala, salt, and black pepper and cook for 1 minute more.
  5. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides, about 5 minutes.
  6. Add the rice and yogurt and stir to coat.
  7. Add 3 cups of water and bring to a boil.
  8. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through.
  9. Remove from heat and let stand for 10 minutes before fluffing with a fork.
  10. Garnish with cilantro, mint, and cashews.

Making fish curry!!!

  1. Marinate the fish: In a medium bowl, combine the fish fillets with 1 teaspoon of the turmeric powder, 1/2 teaspoon of the red chili powder, and 1/4 teaspoon of salt. Set aside for 10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  2. Saut√© the onion: Heat the oil in a large saucepan or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened and translucent, about 5 minutes.
  3. Add the aromatics: Add the garlic, ginger, coriander powder, cumin powder, remaining turmeric powder, and remaining red chili powder to the saucepan. Cook for 30 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant.
  4. Add the spices and tomatoes: Stir in the remaining salt, garam masala, and diced tomatoes. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes, or until the tomatoes have broken down and the mixture has thickened slightly.
  5. Add the coconut milk and fish: Pour in the coconut milk and bring the curry to a low simmer. Nestle the fish fillets into the curry and cook for 5-7 minutes, or until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  6. Garnish and serve: Garnish with chopped cilantro if desired and serve hot with rice or roti.
